The Basics

How long should I stay?

The party itself is one day, but there’ll be a lot of people in the city for the weekend, so expect plenty of impromptu gatherings. Most people are making a long trip of it.

Should we see more of Ireland while we’re over?

If you’ve come this far, you’d be mad not to. The island is small and easy to get around, the north coast (Causeway, Mournes, Glens) is on Belfast’s doorstep. Kerry, Galway, and the west are all within a few hours. Happy to point you somewhere if you want a steer. If you’re interested in a Google maps to help with locations of things, message Andrew to send it on.

Getting There

Can I fly directly to Belfast?

Yes, but it’s not usually the easiest option. Flights into Belfast are often less direct and more expensive than into Dublin. Up to you which way you go.

What currency does Ireland use?

Euro, with Pound sterling in the North. Card is accepted almost everywhere.

Where to Stay

Which neighbourhoods are best?

Cathedral Quarter (the lively one), City Centre (the easy one), Titanic Quarter (the closest to the venue), or Botanic / Queen’s Quarter (the quieter one). Full breakdown is on the Where to Stay page.
