Getting There

How to get to Belfast

Recommended

Flying to Dublin

The best-connected international airport on the island, with direct flights to most of Europe. This is almost always your cheapest, most direct option.

It’s also a good excuse to spend a day or two in Dublin if you fancy it. The city is a 30-minute bus ride from the airport and there’s plenty to do before heading north.

There’s no border to worry about, so travelling from Dublin to Belfast is straightforward. No checks, no signs, you’ll barely even notice you’ve crossed.

When you’re ready to make your way to Belfast, you’ve got three options:

01

Drive

Pick up at car rental Dublin Airport or Dublin City Centre

The flexible option.

Time
~2 hours
Cost
~€75 to €100 per day for economy
Route
M1, it’s a straight road

Best for exploring the coast, staying outside the city, or splitting with mates.

02

Bus

Direct from Dublin Airport and Dublin City Centre

The easy option.

Time
~2 hours
Cost
~€15 one way
Frequency
Hourly

Drops at Belfast’s Europa Buscentre. Book ahead or just turn up.

03

Train

From Dublin City Centre

The cultural option.

Time
~2 hours 15
Cost
~€18 one way
Frequency
8 services a day

Drops at Belfast Lanyon Place, 10 minutes from town. Worth booking ahead.

Quick picks

Travel Suggestions from Dublin Airport

If you already know roughly what you want, here’s the easiest route for each.

Just here for the party

Fly in, party, fly out.

Dublin Airport, Aircoach straight up to Belfast. No faff.

Belfast plus a few days in Dublin

Make a double-header of it.

Bus into Dublin, spend some time there. Then rent a car or take the train and travel north from Dublin City Centre.

Road-tripping the island

Open up the whole country.

Hire a car at Dublin Airport. Causeway Coast, Donegal, Galway, Kerry, all within a few hours. Small island, easiest to get around on four wheels.

Coming as a group of three or more

Just get a car. Honestly.

Better value than splitting tickets, more freedom for day trips, no waiting on timetables.

Drive north. Can’t miss it.

Couldn’t be simpler. Head north, just keep going. You’ll know you’re getting close when you see a field with a horse in it, but not that field, the other field. There’s a big tree on the left, can’t miss it, it’s a beautiful tree.

Anyway, keep going past Jimmy’s, you know Jimmy’s? Big yellow house, used to be blue. Hop onto the motorway around there. If you pass the second services with the Supermac’s you’ve gone too far, but only a small bit, just pull in at the next one and turn yourself around.

Down through Drogheda. Then up through Dundalk. Or the other way round, I never know which is which, they’re both up there together anyway. There’s a turn off somewhere near the second one, ignore it, that’s how you end up in Carrickmacross. Stay on the main road.

You’ll come to a bend on the dual carriageway and there’s a fella usually standing outside a house with a hi-vis on, just give him a nod. Then it’s straight on for a stretch. You’ll start seeing signs for Belfast. Follow them.

If you end up on a ferry to Scotland, you’ve gone too far.
